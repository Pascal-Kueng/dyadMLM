Common Fate Model

library(interdep)

This vignette is a draft and is under construction.

The Common Fate Model (CFM) represents the two members’ reports as indicators of a shared latent dyad-level construct (Ledermann and Kenny 2017; Iida et al. 2018). In the predictor-to-outcome CFM below, the shared predictor level predicts the shared outcome level. The unit-loading paths define each latent variable from the two member reports.

SEM representation of a predictor-to-outcome Common Fate Model. Each pair of member reports loads with a fixed unit loading on a shared latent dyad-level construct. The latent predictor level predicts the latent outcome level. Same-member predictor and outcome residuals may covary, and the latent outcome level has a residual disturbance.

The orange curved paths retain the predictor–outcome association specific to each member rather than assigning it to the shared dyad-level effect. Only the SEM representation is shown here; there is no individual-level multilevel-model diagram for this CFM.

Iida, Masumi, Gwendolyn Seidman, and Patrick E. Shrout. 2018. “Models of Interdependent Individuals Versus Dyadic Processes in Relationship Research.” Journal of Social and Personal Relationships 35 (1): 59–88. https://doi.org/10.1177/0265407517725407.
Ledermann, Thomas, and David A. Kenny. 2017. “Analyzing Dyadic Data with Multilevel Modeling Versus Structural Equation Modeling: A Tale of Two Methods.” Journal of Family Psychology 31 (4): 442–52. https://doi.org/10.1037/fam0000290.
